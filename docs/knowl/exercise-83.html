Exercise 3. Betweenness Centrality on a Tree.

Let \(G\) be an undirected tree on \(N\) vertices. Let \(v\) be a vertex with degree \(k_v = k\text{.}\) Removing the vertex \(v\) splits the tree into \(k \geq 2\) disjoint trees \(G_1,G_2, \ldots, G_k\text{.}\) Suppose that tree \(G_i\) contains \(N_i\) vertices for \(1 \leq i \leq k\text{.}\) Show that the (un-normalized) betweenness centrality of \(v\) satisfies

\begin{equation*} c_v^B = (N-1)^2 - \sum_{i=1}^k N_i^2 \end{equation*}
