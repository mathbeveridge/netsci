Exercise 2.

A fair coin is one such that the outcome of a coin toss has \(\P(H) = \P(T) = \frac{1}{2}\text{,}\) where \(H\) means "heads" and \(T\) means "tails."

  1. A fair coin is tossed repeatedly until two consecutive heads or two consecutive tails appear. Let \(X\) denote the number of coin tosses you make. Find the PMF, the expected value, and the variance of \(X\text{.}\)

  2. Assume now that the coin is tossed until we obtain a tail that is immediately preceded by a head. Find the PMF and the expected value of the number of tosses.
