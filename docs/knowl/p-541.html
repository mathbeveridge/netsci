With probability \(\frac{a}{a+c}\text{,}\) we pick a random vertex. With probability \(\frac{c}{a+c}\text{,}\) we pick a random arc, and then take its target vertex. Among the \(cn\) arcs, \(k_i^{\mathrm{in}}\) of them end at vertex \(i\text{.}\) This gives the same equation

\begin{equation*} \frac{a}{a+c} \cdot \frac{1}{n} + \frac{c}{a+c} \cdot \frac{k_i^{\mathrm{in}}}{cn} \end{equation*}

as in the previous question.
