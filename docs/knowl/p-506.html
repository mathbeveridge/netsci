You will calculate the clustering coefficient

\begin{equation*} C = \frac{3 \times (\mbox{\# of triangles})}{\mbox{\# of connected triples}} \end{equation*}

where a "connected triple" are three vertices \(u,v,w\) such that \((u,v)\) and \((v,w)\) are edges. Intuitively, the clustering coefficient measures the fraction of times that "pairs of my friends are also friends."

  1. Show that there are

    \begin{equation*} n {c/2 \choose 2} \end{equation*}

    triangles in a Ring World. A nice way to argue this is to recognize that all triangles look like one "long edge" going distance \(\leq c/2\) and two "shorter edges." Orient the long edge clockwise the two shorter edges counter clockwise. Then associate this directed cycle with the vertex at the start of the long clockwise arc.

  2. Show that there are

    \begin{equation*} n {c \choose 2} \end{equation*}

    connected triples. These are paths of length 2 (which can be associated with their central vertex).

  3. Show that the clustering coefficient is

    \begin{equation*} C = \frac{3(c-2)}{4(c-1)}. \end{equation*}

    Furthermore, for \(c \geq 3\text{,}\) we have \(\frac{3}{8} \leq C \leq \frac{3}{4}\text{.}\) This is independent of the network size \(n\text{,}\) so we have high clustering.
