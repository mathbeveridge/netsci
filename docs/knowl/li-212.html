Item 10.1.a.

A random variable \(X : \Omega \rightarrow \Z_{\geq 0}\) is a function on the set \(\Omega\) of outcomes of an experiment. We pick a sample outcome and measure one of its property. For example, we could pick a vertex of a network and write down its degree.

This is useful when I want to get a sense of the structure (or typical structure) of a network, or another system.
