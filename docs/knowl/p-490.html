Let's go a step further. Taking the transpose of the equation \(A^{\top} \, A \, \ba= \mu \ba\text{,}\) we get

\begin{equation*} \ba^{\top} A \, A^{\top} = \lambda \ba^{\top}. \end{equation*}

In other words, the row vector \(\ba^{\top}\) is a left eigenvector of the matrix \(A \, A^{\top}\) for the largest eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{.}\) Meanwhile,

\begin{equation*} A \, A^{\top} \, \bh= \lambda \bh \end{equation*}

says that the column vector \(\bh\) is a right eigenvector for the same eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{.}\)
