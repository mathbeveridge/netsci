Pick our constant \(\alpha > 0 \) such that

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{ \kappa_1} < \alpha < \frac{1}{|\kappa_i|} \end{equation*}

for \(2 \leq i \leq n\text{.}\) What can you say about

\begin{equation*} \lim_{t \rightarrow \infty} \by_{1,t} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \lim_{t \rightarrow \infty} \by_{i,t} \end{equation*}

for \(2 \leq i \leq n\text{?}\) You will need to use one (or both) of the following facts about geometric sums and geometric series:

\begin{equation*} 1 + r + r^2 + r^3 + \cdots + r^t = \sum_{k=0}^{t} r^k = \frac{r^{t+1} -1}{r-1} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} 1 + r + r^2 + r^3 + \cdots = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} r^k = \left\{ \begin{array}{cl} \frac{1}{1-r} & \mbox{if } |r| < 1, \\ \infty & \mbox{otherwise}. \end{array} \right. \end{equation*}
