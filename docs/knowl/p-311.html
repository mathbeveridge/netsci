Suppose that \(X\) takes on discrete integer values. Explain why for any \(k \in \Z\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} F_X(k) = \sum_{i=-\infty}^k p_X(i) \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} p_X(k) = F_X(k) - F_X(k-1). \end{equation*}

In other words, if you know the PMF, then you can find the CDF, and vice versa.
