Download this data set, which lists the net worth of the 400 wealthiest Americans in 2002 in millions of dollars.

  1. Create a log-log plot of the CCDF of this data set to confirm that it looks like a power law.

  2. Estimate the value of \(\alpha\) for this data set. Use the maximum likelihood estimamtor for a continuous PDF.

  3. What is the value \(x_{1/2}\) for American wealth in 2002? In other words, if your net worth was at least \(x_{1/2}\text{,}\) then you were in the top half of the population in terms of wealth.

  4. Now find \(w(1/2)\text{.}\) This is the fraction of the wealth in the hands of the wealthiest half of the population.

  5. Finally, find the value \(r^*\) so that \(w(r^*)=1/2\) and its corresponding \(x_{r^*}\text{.}\) The interpretation: the wealthiest \(r^*\) fraction of the population owns half of the total wealth. In order to be among these super-elite people, your net worth must be at least \(x_{r^*}\text{.}\)
