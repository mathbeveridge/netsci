We know that the eignvectors \(\bu_1, \ldots, \bu_n\) form a basis for \(\R^n\) (thanks, Frobenius-Perron!). In particular, we have

\begin{equation*} \bone = c_1 \bu_1 + c_2 \bu_2 + \cdots + c_n \bu_n \end{equation*}

for some choice of \(c_1, \ldots, c_n \in \R\text{.}\) Let us assume that \(c_1 \neq 0\text{.}\) Use parts (a) and (b) to show that the limiting value

\begin{equation*} \bx_t = \alpha A \bx_{t-1} + \bone \end{equation*}

of the Katz recursion is basically proportional to the eigenvector centrality \(\bu_1\text{.}\) In other words, show that

\begin{equation*} \lim_{t \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\bx_t}{| \bx_t |} = \frac{\bu_1}{| \bu_1|}. \end{equation*}
