Let

\begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1\\ 1 & 3 \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}

  1. Check that the vector \(\bv_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) for eigenvalue \(\lambda_1=4\text{.}\)

  2. Check that the vector \(\bv_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \) is an eigenvector of \(A\) for eigenvalue \(\lambda_2 = 2\text{.}\)

  3. Check that \(\bv_1\) and \(\bv_2\) are orthogonal vectors.

  4. Confirm that \(A = \lambda_1 \bu_1 \bu_1^{\top} + \lambda_2 \bu_2 \bu^{\top}\) where \(\bu_1, \bu_2\) are eigenvectors of unit length in the direction of \(\bv_1, \bv_2\) respectively.
