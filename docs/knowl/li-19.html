Item 1.1.7.b.

\((\Rightarrow)\) Suppose that \(T\) is a transitive tournament that contains a cycle. Pick a cycle \(C\) of shortest length on vertices \(v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_k\text{.}\) Note that \(k >3\) since the definition of a transitive tournament forbids a directed 3-cycle. Look at the arc between \(v_1\) and \(v_3\text{.}\) Since \(v_1 v_2\) and \(v_2 v_3\) are arcs, this forces \(v_1 v_3\) to be an arc. But then \(v_1, v_3, \ldots v_k\) is a directed cycle whose length is shorter than \(C\text{,}\) a contradiction. Therefore \(T\) cannot contain any directed cycles.

\((\Leftarrow)\) Suppose that \(T\) has no directed cycles. Suppose that \((u,v)\) and \((v,w)\) are arcs. We cannot have a directed 3-cycle, so \(u\) and \(w\) must be connected by the arc \((u,w)\text{.}\) Therefore, \(T\) is transitive.
