Item 18.3.e.ii.

Consider a graph whose degree distribution obeys a power law

\begin{equation*} \Pr[\deg(v)=q] \sim q^{-\alpha}. \end{equation*}

For a natural power law, we have \(2 < \alpha < 3\text{.}\) Recall that for a natural power law, \(\langle k \rangle\) is finite but \(\langle k^2 \rangle\) is infinite. (This is what led to the appearance of hubs.) What does this imply about the infection rate on such a network? What role must the hubs play, especially in light of part (d) above, and the fact that "your friends are more popular than you are"?
