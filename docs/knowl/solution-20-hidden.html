Let's factor out \(\lambda_1^n\) from this expression to get

\begin{align*} A^n \bx &= \lambda_1^n \left( c_1 \bu_1 + c_2 \left(\frac{ \lambda_2}{\lambda_1}^n \right) \bu_2. \right) \end{align*}

As \(n\) gets really large, the fraction \((\lambda_2/\lambda_1)^n\) goes to zero. Therefore for large \(n\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} A^n \bx \approx \lambda_1^n c_1 \bu_1. \end{equation*}
