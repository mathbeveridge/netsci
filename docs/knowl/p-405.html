For many real-world networks, the power law behavior doesn't hold for small \(k\text{.}\) In this case, we restrict our attention to vertices of degree at least \(k_{\min}\text{.}\) We then deal with the probability mass function for a truncated power law. Given \(\alpha\) and \(k \geq k_{\min}\) this PMF is given by

\begin{equation*} p(k) = \frac{ k^{-\alpha}}{\zeta(\alpha, k_{\min})} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} \zeta(x, k_{\min}) = \sum_{k=k_{\min}}^{\infty} k^{-x} \end{equation*}

is the generalized Riemann zeta function.
