In this part, you will show that when \(n\) is very large and \(p\) is very small, the Poisson PMF for

\begin{equation*} \lambda :=np \end{equation*}

is a good approximation (for small \(k\)) of the binomial PMF with parameters \(n\) and \(p\text{.}\) We will fix \(k \in \Z_{\geq 0}\) to be constant and let \(n \rightarrow \infty\text{.}\) You will show that

\begin{equation*} {n \choose k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} \approx e^{-\lambda} \frac{\lambda^k}{k!} \mbox{if } k \ll n. \end{equation*}
