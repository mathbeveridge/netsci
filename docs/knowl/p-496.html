Let \(A\) be the adjacency matrix for a connected, undirected and aperiodic graph. Recall that the Frobenius-Perron Theorem guarantees that the symmetric matrix \(A\) has real eigenvalues \(\kappa_1 \geq \kappa_2 \geq \cdots \geq \kappa_n\) with corresponding orthonormal eigenvectors \(\bu_1, \bu_2, \ldots , \bu_n\text{.}\) Furthermore

  • The eigenvalue \(\kappa_1\) has multiplicity one, and \(\kappa_1 > | \kappa_i |\) for \(2 \leq i \leq n\text{.}\)

  • The corresponding eigenvector \(\bu_1\) has non-negative entries.

In this problem, you will show that when \(n \geq 3\text{,}\) we have \(\kappa_1 \geq 1\text{.}\)

  1. Let \(\bx = A \bone\text{.}\) Explain explain why \(\bx \geq \bone\text{,}\) (meaning \(x_i \geq 1\) for all \(i\)), and explain why there is at least one \(j\) such that \(x_j > 1\text{.}\)

  2. The orthonormal eigenvectors \(\bu_1, \bu_2, \ldots , \bu_n\) form a basis for \(\R^n\text{.}\) So there exist \(c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_n\) such that

    \begin{equation*} \bone = c_1 \bu_1 + c_2 \bu_2 + \cdots + c_n \bu_n. \end{equation*}

    Consider

    \begin{equation*} A \bone = A(c_1 \bu_1 + c_2 \bu_2 + \cdots + c_n \bu_n). \end{equation*}

    Explain why \(\kappa_1 < 1\) leads to a contradiction to your observations in part (a).
