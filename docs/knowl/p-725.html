Suppose that we have \(n\) vertices and \(m\) edges. Let's think of our graph as "unwired" meaning that a vertex of degree \(k\) is a vertex with \(k\) edge stubs (which connect randomly to one another). We start at a vertex \(v\) and then pick a random stub connected to vertex \(v\text{.}\) This stub is equally likely to connect to any of the other stub. So the probability that we connect to a particular vertex of degree \(k+1\) is

\begin{equation*} \frac{k+1}{2m-1} \approx \frac{k+1}{2m}. \end{equation*}

The number of vertices of degree \(k+1\) is \(p_{k+1} n\text{.}\) So the chance that we randomly connect to a vertex with excess degree \(k\) is

\begin{equation*} q_k = \frac{k+1}{2m} \cdot p_{k+1} n = \frac{n}{2m} \cdot (k+1) p_{k+1} = \frac{(k+1) p_{k+1} }{\langle k \rangle}. \end{equation*}
