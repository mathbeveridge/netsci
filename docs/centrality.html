Chapter 2 Centrality Measures

Here are the network and centrality definitions that we will need.

  • Let \(G=(V,E)\) be a network (graph) on \(N\) vertices.

  • Let \(k_i = \deg(i)\) be the degree of vertex \(i\)

  • Let \(d_{i,j} = d(i,j)\) be the distance between vertices \(i\) and \(j\)

  • Let \(n_{s,t}\) be the number of geodesics (shortest paths) from \(s\) to \(t\)

  • Let \(n_{s,t}(i)\) be the number of \((s,t)\)-geodesics that go through vertex \(i\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline \mbox{name} & \mbox{formula} & \mbox{normalized formula} \\ \hline \hline &&\\ \mbox{Degree Centrality} & c_i^D = \deg(i) = k_i & \displaystyle{\frac{c_i^D}{N-1}} \\ &&\\ \hline &&\\ \mbox{Betweenness Centrality} & c_i^B = \displaystyle{\sum_{s \neq i} \sum_{t \neq i, s} \frac{n_{s,t}(i)}{n_{s,t}}} & \displaystyle{\frac{c_i^B}{{(N-1)(N-2)}}} \\ &&\\ \hline &&\\ \mbox{Closeness Centrality} & c_i^C = \displaystyle{\frac{1}{\sum_{j} d_{i,j}}} & \displaystyle{ C_i^C = (N-1) \, c_i^C }\\ && \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

2. Centrality Calculation Examples.

For each of the following graphs, calculate the (unnormalized) degree centrality, betweenness centrality and closeness centrality

  1. Degree Centrality \(c_i^D = \deg(i) = k_i\)

  2. Betweenness Centrality \(c_i^B = \displaystyle{\sum_{s \neq i} \sum_{t \neq i, s} \frac{n_{s,t}(i)}{n_{s,t}}}\)

  3. Closeness Centrality \(c_i^C = \displaystyle{\frac{1}{\sum_{j} d_{i,j}}}\)

4. Digraph Centrality Measures.
