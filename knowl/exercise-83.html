Exercise 4. Counting Lazy Random Walks.

In a lazy walk on a directed graph, at each step, we can either stay at the current vertex or move to an adjacent vertex. So a lazy walk of length \(k\) is a sequence of vertices \(v_0, v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_k\) where for \(1 \leq i \leq k\text{,}\) we either have \(v_i = v_{i-1}\) or \((v_{i-1}, v_i)\) is an arc of \(G\text{.}\) Let \(A\) be the adjacency matrix of a directed graph \(G\text{,}\) so that \(a_{ij}\) is 1 if \((i,j)\) is an arc and is 0 otherwise. Using induction on \(k \geq 0\text{,}\) prove that the \(ij\)th entry of \((I+A)^k\) counts the number of lazy walks of length \(k\) from vertex \(i\) to vertex \(j\text{.}\)
