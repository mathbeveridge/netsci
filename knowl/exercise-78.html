Exercise 5. Directed rooted trees.

Let \(G=(V,E)\) be a tree and let \(v \in V\text{.}\) To obtain a directed tree rooted at vertex \(v\text{,}\) we orient the edges so that they point away from the vertex \(v\text{.}\) In other words, there is a directed path from \(v\) to every other vertex in the tree.

Prove that if \(D = (V,E)\) is a strongly connected digraph, then for every \(v \in V\text{,}\) the digraph contains a directed tree \(T = (V_T, E_T)\) rooted at \(v\) such that \(V_T = V\text{.}\)

(Note: A subgraph that includes every vertex of the original graph is called a spanning subgraph. Spanning trees (of graphs) and spanning directed trees (of digraphs) have many important applications.)
