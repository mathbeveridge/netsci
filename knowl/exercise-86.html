Exercise 7. A PageRank Variant.
\begin{equation*} \bx = \alpha P^{\top} \bx + \frac{(1 - \alpha)}{n} \bone \end{equation*}

where \(P\) is the transition matrix for a random walk on the network. In this problem, you will consider a variant of this equation. Let \(S\) be any fixed set of \(m\) vertices and let \(\overline{S}\) be the \(n-m\) remaining vertices. Given a fixed (constant) vector \(\bb_S \in \R^m\) on the set \(S\text{,}\) we want to solve

\begin{equation*} \left[ \begin{array}{c} \bb_S \\ \bx_{\overline{S}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{cc} I_{S,S} & \bzero_{S,\overline{S}} \\ \alpha P_{S,\overline{S}}^{\top} & \alpha P_{\overline{S}, \overline{S}}^{\top} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \bb_S \\ \bx_{\overline{S}} \end{array} \right] + \frac{(1 - \alpha)}{| \overline{S}|} \left[ \begin{array}{c} \bzero_S \\ \bone_{\overline{S}} \end{array} \right] \end{equation*}

for the unknown vector \(\bx_{\overline{S}} \in \R^{n-m}\) where

  • \(I_{S,S}\) is an \(m \times m\) identity matrix

  • \(\bzero_{S, \overline{S}}\) is an \(m \times (n-m)\) zero matrix.

  • \(P_{S,\overline{S}}\) is the \(m \times (n-m)\) matrix describing the transition probabilities from \(S\) to \(\overline{S}\)

  • \(P_{\overline{S},\overline{S}}\) is the \((n-m) \times (n-m)\) matrix describing the transition probabilities from \(\overline{S}\) to \(\overline{S}\)

  • \(\bzero_S\) is the \(m\)-dimensional all-zeros vector

  • \(\bone_{\overline{S}}\) is the \((n-m)\)-dimensional all-ones vector

  • \(\alpha \in [0,1]\text{.}\)

Given what you know about (standard) PageRank, interpret the "update rule" that this equation calculates. Then describe a web searching example where using this equation produces a helpful ranking.
