Given a directed graph (digraph) \(D=(V,E)\) is acyclic, a directed cycle of size \(k\) is a set of arcs \(\)(v_1, v_2), (v_2, v_3), \ldots (v_{k-1}, v_k), (v_k, v_1)\(\) such that \(v_1\) is the only vertex that repeats. An acyclic digraph \(D\) has no directed cycles for any \(k \geq 1\text{.}\) Prove that if \(D\) is an acyclic digraph, then \(D\) contains at least one source vertex \(i\) with \(\kin_i=0\) and at least one sink vertex \(j\) with \(\kout_j=0\text{.}\)
