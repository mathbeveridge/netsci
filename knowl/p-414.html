For \(t \in \Z_{\geq 0}\text{,}\) the value \(p_X(t)\) is the probability that \(X=t\) when we perform our experiment. For example, the PMF of "measure the degree of a randomly chosen vertex" is

\begin{equation*} p(t) = \frac{\mbox{number of vertices of degree }}{\mbox{total number of vertices}} \end{equation*}
