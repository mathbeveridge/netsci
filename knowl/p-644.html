Let \(f: [n] \rightarrow [n]\) be any function from the set \([n] = \{ 1,2,3, \ldots, n \}\) to itself. We can use this function to create a digraph \(D=([n], E)\) where the set of arcs is

\begin{equation*} E = \{ (i, f(i)) : i \in [n] \}. \end{equation*}

Note that when \(f(i)=i\text{,}\) we get a loop (which is a directed cycle of length 1).

  1. Draw the digraph for the function \(f: [8] \rightarrow [8]\) given by

    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|cccccccc} k & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 & 7 & 8 \\ \hline f(k) & 6 & 4 & 3 & 8 & 4 & 1 & 8 & 2 \end{array} \end{equation*}

  2. Explain why \(\kout_i=1\) for every vertex \(i\text{.}\)

  3. Prove that \(D\) consists of a collection of disjoint unicyclic digraphs, meaning that each weakly connected component contains exactly one directed cycle. (Hint: assume for the sake of contradiction that some weakly connected component has two directed cycles. There are two cases: (1) the cycles share one or more vertices and (2) the cycles are disjoint.)
