The converse is also true: if every pair of vertices in \(G\) is connected by two edge-disjoint paths, then there is a strongly connected orientation \(D\) of the graph. Here is the sketch of an algorithm that orients the edges of \(G\) to obtain a strongly connected digraph.

  1. Start with any two vertices \(u,v\) and orient some edges so that they are strongly connected to one another. Let \(S = \{ u , v\}\text{.}\)

  2. Pick a vertex \(w \notin S\text{.}\) If \(w\) and \(v\) are not strongly connected, then orient some edges to make this happen. Add \(w\) to \(S\text{.}\)

  3. Repeat the previous step until \(S = V\text{,}\) which means that every vertex is strongly connected to \(v\text{.}\)

  4. If there any any non-oriented edges, give them an orientation.
