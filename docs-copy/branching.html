Chapter 24 The Branching Process

In this activity, we will explore a simple model that creates a family tree. Later, we will adapt this argument to understand the threshold for the giant component of a random graph. We let \(X_t\) denote the size of the \(t\)-th generation in the family tree.

  • At time \(t=0\text{,}\) there is one individual. So \(X_0=1\text{.}\)

  • At time \(t=1\text{,}\) she gives birth to \(X_1\) children and dies.

  • At time \(t >2\text{,}\) each of the \(X_{t-1}\) individuals in generation \(t-1\) give birth and die. This results in a total of \(X_t\) children in the \(t\)-th generation.

Here is an example of the branching process.

The number of children \(Y\) produced by each individual is an independent random variable governed by (the same) probability distribution. The expected number of children produced is

\begin{equation*} \E[Y] = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} k \P[Y=k] = \mu. \end{equation*}

The goal of this activity will be to show that \(\mu\) determines the probability of whether the family tree dies out after a finite number of generations, or if it grows infinitely large.

Exercises Practice Problems

1. Expected Size of Generation \(n\).

Use induction on \(t \geq 0\) to prove that \(X_n\text{,}\) the size of the \(n\)-th generation satisfies

\begin{equation*} \E[X_n] = \mu^n \end{equation*}
2. Expected Size of the Tree After \(n\) Generations.

Let \(\displaystyle{Z_n= \sum_{t=0}^{n} X_t}\) be the total number of individuals in the family tree at time \(n\text{.}\)

  1. Use linearity of expectation to prove that

    \begin{equation*} \E[Z_n] = 1 + \mu + \mu^2 + \cdots + \mu^n. \end{equation*}

  2. Confirm that the formula in the previous problem is equivalent to

    \begin{equation*} \E[Z_n] = \frac{\mu^{n+1} -1}{\mu -1}. \end{equation*}
3. Asymptotic Size of the Tree.

Now let's look at the asymptotic behavior of the size of the tree. Let

\begin{equation*} \E [Z] = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \E[Z_n] = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\mu^{n+1} -1}{\mu -1}. \end{equation*}

  1. Confirm that if \(\mu < 1\) then \(\E[Z]\) is finite. Then find a formula for \(\E[Z]\text{.}\)

  2. Confirm that if \(\mu > 1\) then \(\E[Z]\) is infinite.

4. Threshold for the Branching Process.

We say that \(\mu =1\) is a threshold for whether the family tree dies out. This is because

  • if \(\E[Z]\) is finite then \(\P[ \mbox{there exists } N \mbox{ such that } X_N = 0]=1\text{.}\)

Show that this is true by proving the contrapositive statement:

  • if \(\P[ X_t \geq 1 \mbox{ for all t} ]=\alpha > 0\) then \(\E[Z]\) is infinite.
