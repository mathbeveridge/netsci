Exercise 4. The Degree Distribution of \(G(n,p)\).

Explain why the probability that vertex \(v\) of \(G(n,p)\) has degree \(k\) is given by

\begin{equation*} p_k = {n-1 \choose k} p^k (1-p)^{n-1-k}. \end{equation*}

In other words, the degree of a vertex is a random variable whose PMF is the binomial distribution. Therefore we say that \(G(n,p)\) has a "binomial degree distribution." Solution.

Notes about the binomial distribution:

  • See Exercise 10.2 for the basics about the binomial distribution.

  • See Exercise 10.5 to see how we can approximate the binomial distribution with a Poisson distribtuion. Namely, when \(k\) is very small, \(n\) is very large and \(p= c/n\text{,}\) we have

    \begin{equation*} p_k \approx e^{-c} \frac{c^k}{k!}. \end{equation*}
