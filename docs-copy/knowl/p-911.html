For any \(k \in \Z\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \overline{F}_X(k) = \sum_{i=k+1}^{\infty} p_X(i) \end{equation*}

by the definition of a CCDF. We then have

\begin{equation*} \overline{F}_X(k-1) - \overline{F}_X(k)= \sum_{i=k}^{\infty} p_X(i) - \sum_{i=k+1}^{\infty} p_X(i) = p_X(k). \end{equation*}

In other words, if you know the PMF, then you can find the CCDF, and vice versa.
